## Every inconsistency/contradiction found, and what I did

1. **The three-way collision** between ABI wavelet, replace-the-interpreter,
   and Stage 3's remaining boxed-backend items — all rewrite or extend
   emit.rs's representation. Resolved by ordering: decisions → types → WIT
   surface → ABI backend → interpreter retirement. Boxed-backend Stage 3 work
   (f32, flags, stdlib-parity builtins, u64 workaround) is deliberately NOT
   finished in the boxed regime. **This re-sequences your instruction in
   Round-trip update 006** ("continue stage 3, then stage 2") — the biggest
   call in this re-plan; veto it if you want the matrix green sooner at the
   cost of double implementation.
2. **GC contradiction**: "Implement garbage collection" (wasm-GC over tagged
   boxes) would be undone by the header-less canonical layout, and wasm-GC
   can't hold Canonical-ABI linear-memory values (at most the closure
   residue). Folded into 5.1, which its own text said must be decided before
   layout work.
3. **Replace-interpreter's "GC first" prerequisite is internally
   inconsistent** with its own Step 2 design (fresh module instance per REPL
   entry bounds leaks per-entry). Dropped; memory story comes from 5.1.
4. **Stdlib split existed three times** (top-level Thing, F1 prerequisite,
   "monomorphize the higher-order stdlib") plus the open question about
   pullable stdlib variants. Unified at 5.7 so the stdlib is written in
   Wavelet once, typed/monomorphic from the start; variants stay parked (8.3).
5. **u64 range needed no standalone design decision**: it's an artifact of
   boxing every int as i64. Dissolves into 3.4 (typed literals) + 5.2
   (per-width unboxing); seed B's u64::MAX check joins the 5.12 gate.
6. **Three things were one closure question** (HOF-as-libraries, Closures
   over the wire, defunctionalization). Now: 2.3 decides (your requested
   options-and-recommendation write-up, no implementation), 5.8 implements,
   8.2 keeps over-the-wire as the follow-on it enables.
7. **"Implement boundary coercions and a safely wrapper" contradicts 2.2.4**
   (drop runtime The checks and checked coercions). Rescoped to a residual
   `safely` for genuinely-dynamic data (read input, lying foreign
   components); parked at 8.4 until 2.2 lands.
8. **"Confine runtime meta ops" duplicated "Drop or confine runtime eval"** —
   merged into 2.2.5.
9. **F2 and F5 each existed twice** (plan-index children vs Step-2 children,
   near-verbatim). Duplicates retired under 6.2/6.3.
10. **User-declared resources existed twice** (general + conformance-counter
    scoped). General wins (4.5); the counter is its acceptance test.
11. **`wavelet run` replacement existed twice** (Build/CLI gap + Step-2
    rider). Merged at 6.5.
12. **Code review vs the ABI rewrite**: reviewing then rewriting emit.rs's
    representation machinery is wasted motion. 2.1 rescoped to surviving code
    + simplification proposals feeding 2.2–2.4; emit.rs findings become goal-5
    inputs. (The stale check.rs header comment noted in "Update the prose
    surfaces" folds into 2.1.)
13. **Docs tasks parked** under 8.1 per your update 002, including "Update
    the prose surfaces" (its design.md §3 rewrite will be forced by goal 3
    anyway; CLAUDE.md's finish-the-surfaces rule still applies per-change).
14. **Large sum/flag limits** (>256 cases, >32 members) are boxed-encoding
    properties, not features — now a design constraint recorded on 5.4.
15. Exhaustiveness checking was "at most a lint" with no home — scheduled as
    3.12, where it becomes cheap (checker models variants, Match is static).
