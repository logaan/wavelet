**Created 2.7 "Decide grouping and the meaning of (a)"**
(lot:033ichoJaN3hdjhyPgjlVj), with question, options, and a recommendation
informed by your comments. One thing worth knowing up front: the language
*already* reads `(a)` as a zero-argument call — the lisp way you describe.
`( ... )` uniformly builds a tuple (`(a)` is `Tup[a]`), and in evaluation
position a tuple is a call; this is implemented in reader.rs and documented
in syntax.mdx and reader-sugar.mdx. "Pure grouping" was only ever update
002's proposal here, not the status quo. 2.7's recommendation is accordingly
to keep the current semantics and close with no language change.

**Created 2.8 "Decide whether If moves to the stdlib"**
(lot:033ichxG3KqARl4pfnTemv). Recommendation: yes — `If c t e` expands to
`Match c [(true t) (false e)]`, preserving laziness, and its typing comes
free from Match's — but sequence it behind 3.6 (check macro-expanded code)
and 3.12 (exhaustiveness) so If's diagnostics don't regress in the interim.
`Do` doesn't need to wait; it has no checker involvement.
