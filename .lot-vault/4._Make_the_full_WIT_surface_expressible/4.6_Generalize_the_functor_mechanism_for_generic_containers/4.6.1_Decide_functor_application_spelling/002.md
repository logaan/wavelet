**Question: how is functor application spelled — a mode of `Import`, or its
own form — and how do multi-parameter functors pass their arguments?**

Options:

1. **A dedicated `Instantiate` form with named arguments (recommended).**
   `Import` stays what it is — "bring an unparameterized dependency into
   scope". Application is its own explicit operation, e.g.
   `Instantiate {pkg: ... with: {elem: t len: 3} as: ...}`. Named arguments
   scale from one parameter to many, self-document at the call site, and echo
   the component model's own `with`-style instantiation, so the surface matches
   what it lowers to.
2. **Overload `Import`** (`Import {pkg: ... elem: t as: ...}`, the current
   sketch). One fewer form, but it conflates two different operations —
   resolving a dependency and applying a functor to mint a new instance — and
   in the option map nothing distinguishes functor parameters from import
   options, which degrades into undifferentiated key soup as parameters
   multiply.
3. **Both** — keep `Import`-with-args as sugar for the single-parameter case
   over a general `Instantiate`. Two spellings for one operation; skip.

Recommendation: **option 1.** On the multi-parameter sub-question: arguments
match functor parameters *by name* (a `with:` map), not positionally —
parameters in the functor's signature are named, and positional application
breaks silently under parameter reordering.
